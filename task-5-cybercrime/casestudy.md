# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

## Overview
UPI payment fraud is a common type of cybercrime where scammers trick individuals into transferring money through digital payment applications.

## How the Crime Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The scammer contacts the victim via call, message, or social media.
2. They pretend to be a buyer or customer support agent.
3. The scammer sends a "collect request" instead of sending money.
4. The victim receives a payment request on their UPI app.
5. The victim is misled into thinking they are receiving money.
6. The victim enters their UPI PIN to approve the request.
7. Money gets transferred from the victim’s account to the scammer.

## Targeted Victims
- Students
- Elderly people
- Individuals unfamiliar with digital payments

## Consequences
- Financial loss
- Stress and anxiety
- Possible exposure of banking details
- Difficulty in recovering lost money

## Conclusion
UPI fraud can be prevented by staying alert. Users should never share their UPI PIN, always verify payment requests, and avoid clicking on unknown links or scanning suspicious QR codes.

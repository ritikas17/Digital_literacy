## Part A: Professional Emails

### Why Email Etiquette Matters
Email is the foundation of professional communication. Whether reaching out to a professor,
a recruiter, or a collaborator, a well-written email reflects maturity, clarity, and respect.
In academic and professional settings, how you communicate is just as important as what
you communicate.

### Rules for a Perfect Professional Email
- **Subject Line** — Clear and specific, telling the reader exactly what the email is about
- **Greeting** — Respectful and addressed correctly (e.g. "Dear Professor X" not "Hey")
- **Body** — Short, to the point, and free of unnecessary detail
- **Language** — Formal and grammatically correct, no abbreviations like "u", "plz", or "thx"
- **Closing** — Polite sign-off like "Regards" or "Thank you", followed by your full name
- **Response Time** — Replying within 24 hours is considered good professional practice

## Part B: Social Media Safety Awareness

### Why Social Media Safety Awareness Matters
Social media has become an inseparable part of student life — used for communication,
networking, learning, and self-expression. However, with this constant connectivity
comes a set of risks that are often underestimated. Cyberbullying, identity theft,
misinformation, and reputational damage are just some of the consequences of careless
social media use, and they can have very real and lasting effects on a student's
personal and professional life.

Awareness is the first line of defense. Most students who fall victim to online risks
do so not out of negligence, but simply because they were never taught what to look
out for. Understanding what to share, what to avoid, how to protect privacy settings,
and how to verify information before spreading it are skills that are just as important
as any academic subject in today's digital world.

Building good social media habits early in college also shapes long-term professional
reputation. Recruiters and institutions increasingly look at candidates' online presence,
and a single inappropriate post can undo years of hard work. On the flip side, a clean,
thoughtful, and professional social media profile can actively work in a student's favor.
